You are receiving a stream of 2D points representing GPS coordinates projected to a plane. The points arrive in batches of size B (no deletions). After each batch, compute the area of the convex hull of all points seen so far. Propose a practical data structure and update strategy that (i) preprocesses efficiently, (ii) updates amortized per batch, (iii) yields hull area in O(1) or O(log h), (iv) uses near-linear space, (v) includes two optimizations, and (vi) demonstrates how this compares to rebuilding the hull from scratch after every batch. Provide asymptotics in terms of n (total points) and B?
