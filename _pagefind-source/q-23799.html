Scenario: You maintain N time-intervals [s_i, e_i) on a discrete timeline 0..P-1, representing user-active windows. Updates arrive in batches of B that change either s_i by ds or e_i by de, always preserving s_i < e_i. After each batch, determine if there exists a time t with at least K overlapping intervals. Propose a data structure and an amortized update strategy with: (i) preprocessing in O(N+P), (ii) batch updates in O(B log P), (iii) query in O(1), (iv) near-linear space, (v) two optimizations, (vi) a comparison to recomputing overlaps from scratch. Provide asymptotics in N, B, P, K?
