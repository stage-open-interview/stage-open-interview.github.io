You have a dynamic set P of n 2D points added in batches of B. After each batch, compute the size of the Pareto frontier (points not dominated by any other: a dominates b if x_a ≥ x_b and y_a ≥ y_b with at least one strict). Propose a practical data structure to maintain the frontier with amortized update time per batch, O(1) frontier-size queries, near-linear space, and two optimizations. Compare to rebuilding after every batch. Provide asymptotics in n and B?
