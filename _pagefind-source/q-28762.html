You maintain a binary array b[1..n]. In each batch of B updates, you toggle b[i] for various i. After a batch, determine if there exists a contiguous run of at least length L consisting of 1s. Propose a data structure that (i) preprocesses in O(n), (ii) processes each batch in amortized O(B log n), (iii) answers in O(1) whether such a run exists, (iv) uses near-linear space, (v) two optimizations, and (vi) comparisons to recomputing from scratch after every batch. Provide asymptotics in n, B, L?
