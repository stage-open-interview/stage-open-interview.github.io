You maintain an undirected graph G=(V,E) with n nodes; edges arrive in batches of size B (no deletions). After each batch, count the number of unordered pairs {u,v} with dist(u,v) ≤ 2. Propose a practical incremental data structure that uses near-linear space and supports updates in amortized time O(B * T) with a tunable threshold T, plus two optimizations (high-degree handling and lazy rebuild). Provide asymptotics in terms of n, m, B, and T?
