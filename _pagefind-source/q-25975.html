Your team observes slow queries: (1) 'SELECT * FROM orders WHERE customerId = ? AND status = ? AND createdAt > ? ORDER BY createdAt DESC LIMIT 20' averages 800ms, and (2) 'SELECT status, COUNT(*) FROM orders WHERE createdAt > NOW() - INTERVAL '30 days' GROUP BY status' takes 12 seconds. The database runs on a 16-core, 64GB RAM server with SSD. The orders table receives ~5000 writes/sec and serves ~8000 reads/sec. How would you design an indexing strategy to address both query patterns, and what are the tradeoffs of each index you create?
