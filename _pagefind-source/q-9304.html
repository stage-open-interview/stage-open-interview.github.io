Dynamic top-k sum under batched updates: You maintain n users with scores s[i]. Online batches of B updates set s[i] to new values (each i may repeat across batches; at most one per update). After each batch, output sum of the largest k scores among all users. Propose a practical data structure and amortized analysis: initialization, per-batch update cost, memory, two optimizations, and a comparison to rebuilding from scratch after every batch. Provide asymptotics in n, B, k?
