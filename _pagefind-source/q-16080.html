In a streaming analytics pipeline for ad clicks, events arrive in batches of size B. You must maintain a sliding window of the last W events and after each batch output the top-3 most frequent user_ids in the window. Design a data structure that supports per-event insertions and evictions with amortized O(log n) time and O(n) memory, while answering the top-3 in O(1). Provide a tight amortized analysis and compare to recomputing frequencies from scratch after every batch. Ignore deletions other than evictions?
