You maintain an array a[1..n] of integers. Online, you receive batches of B point updates (i, v) changing a[i] to v. After each batch, output the number of local inversions with distance at most d, i.e., pairs (i, j) where i<j, j-i <= d, and a[i] > a[j]. Propose a practical structure with preprocessing, amortized update time, memory bounds, two optimizations, and a comparison to recomputing all such pairs after every batch. Provide asymptotics in n, d, B?
