Scenario: An e-commerce platform has a PostgreSQL orders table with 50M rows (orderId, customerId, status, createdAt, totalAmount, category). The analytics team runs three frequent queries: Q1) SELECT * FROM orders WHERE status = 'pending' AND createdAt > NOW() - INTERVAL '7 days'; Q2) SELECT * FROM orders WHERE customerId = ? AND createdAt > NOW() - INTERVAL '30 days' ORDER BY createdAt DESC; Q3) SELECT customerId, SUM(totalAmount) FROM orders WHERE category = ? AND createdAt BETWEEN ? AND ? GROUP BY customerId. With 10% pending orders, 1000 daily new customers, and 50 categories, design an optimal indexing strategy: 1) Which indexes are needed and why? 2) What is the optimal column ordering for composite indexes? 3) Should you use partial/filtered indexes? 4) How would you verify index effectiveness with EXPLAIN ANALYZE? 5) What are the write amplification trade-offs?
