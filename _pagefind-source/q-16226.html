Maintain a dynamic convex hull via batches: points arrive in batches of B; after each batch, output the perimeter of the convex hull of all points seen so far. Propose a practical incremental convex hull data structure that (i) supports batched inserts efficiently, (ii) updates the hull in amortized O(log n) per point, (iii) reports the hull perimeter in O(1), (iv) uses near-linear space, (v) includes two optimizations, and (vi) compares to rebuilding the hull after every batch. Provide asymptotics in terms of n=total points and B?
