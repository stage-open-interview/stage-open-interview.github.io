In a two-world Kripke model for modal logic K with two agents A and B and propositional vars p, q, r, use Ba, Bb for beliefs. Standard semantics: Ba φ true at w iff φ true in all A-accessible worlds from w, Bb φ similarly. Given constraints: Ba p → Bb p; Bb q → Ba q; (p ∨ q) → Ba r. Does a model with exactly two worlds {w0,w1}, w0 designated, exist where p is true in w0, q is false in w0, and Bb r is false in all B-accessible worlds from w0? Outline construction?
