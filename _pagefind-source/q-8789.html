Suppose a directed graph with N nodes and non-negative weights is fixed with a source s. Online, edges are added and weights updated in batches of size B. After each batch, count how many vertices have dist(s, v) increased by more than delta since the previous batch. Propose a practical hybrid scheme: (i) incremental relax passes limited to the affected region, (ii) periodic full Dijkstra every T batches, (iii) a lazy rebuild trigger when incremental cost exceeds a bound. Provide preprocessing, amortized per-batch update time, memory, two optimizations, and a comparison to re-running Dijkstra after every batch. Analyze asymptotics in n, m, B, T?
