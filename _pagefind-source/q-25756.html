You manage a dynamic undirected graph with N nodes and M potential edges. Edges are toggled in batches of size B (insert or delete; deletions guaranteed to exist). After each batch, determine whether the graph contains at least one triangle (3-cycle). Propose a practical data structure that (i) initializes in O(N+M), (ii) updates in amortized O(B sqrt M) using a heavy/light degree partition and bitsets, (iii) answers existence in O(1), (iv) uses near-linear space, (v) two optimizations, and (vi) compares to recomputing from scratch. Provide asymptotics in N, M, B?
