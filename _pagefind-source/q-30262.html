Consider three variables F1, F2, F3 in {T, F, U}. Two rules: (F1 ∧ F2) → F3, and (¬F1) ∨ F2 → F3. Using Kleene 3-valued logic (¬ flips T/F, U→U; ∧/∨ as min/max with T>U>F). Is there an assignment with exactly one U that makes both rules not evaluate to F? Outline a minimal forward-chaining validator and provide a concrete satisfying assignment?
