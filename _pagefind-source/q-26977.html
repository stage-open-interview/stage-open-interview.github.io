In 2D, a growing set P of points is received strictly by appending batches of B points. You must support: (a) batch insertion of B points, (b) queries whether a given point q lies inside the current convex hull of P in O(log h) time, where h is the hull size, and (c) reporting the hull vertices. Design a practical data structure that maintains the hull with amortized work per batch, derive the asymptotics as n = |P| grows and B varies, provide two optimizations, and compare the performance to rebuilding the hull from scratch after every batch?
