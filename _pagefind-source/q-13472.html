Consider predicates P1..P4 over a finite domain {d1,d2} with values in {T,F,U} under Kleene 3-valued semantics. Extend the standard three-valued rules to quantified constraints: 1) For all x in {d1,d2}, (P1(x) ∧ P2(x)) → P3(x) 2) Exists x in {d1,d2}, (P2(x) ∨ P4(x)) → P1(x) 3) For all x, (¬P4(x)) → (P1(x) ∨ P3(x)) Is there an assignment with at least one U such that none of the instantiated constraints evaluates to F? Outline a backtracking propagation and give a concrete satisfying assignment?
