In a 4-variable policy puzzle, V1..V4 ∈ {T, F, U}. Constraints: (V1 ∨ V2) → V3, (V2 ∧ V4) → ¬V1, and (¬V3) → (V1 ∨ V4). Is there an assignment with at least one U such that none of the constraints evaluates to F? Outline a simple 3-valued constraint-propagation strategy and give a concrete satisfying assignment?
