In a hardware verification context, model-checking puzzle: Given 5 boolean signals S1..S5 each can be T/F/U (Kleene three-valued). Semantics: ¬ maps T<->F, U->U; ∧/∨ as min/max with T > U > F. Define the constraint set: C1: (S1 ∧ ¬S2) → S3, C2: (S2 ∨ S4) ∧ ¬S5, C3: (S3 ∨ S5) → S1, C4: (¬S1) ∨ (S4 ∧ ¬S3). Is there an assignment with at least one U such that none of the constraints evaluates to F? Outline a backtracking propagation algorithm and provide a concrete satisfying assignment?
