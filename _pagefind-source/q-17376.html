You monitor a set of n routes with a live travel-time value t[i]. Online you receive batches of B updates changing t[i] to a new value. After each batch, output the sum of the K smallest travel times (K fixed, e.g., 50). Propose a practical data structure that exactly maintains the K smallest values, handles updates amortized, and uses near-linear memory. Include preprocessing, two optimizations, and a comparison to recomputing K smallest from scratch after every batch. Provide asymptotics in n, B, K?
