Imagine an array a[1..n] of integers. You receive online batches of B point updates (a[i] = v). After each batch you must output the maximum subarray sum of the current array. Propose a practical data structure that (i) maintains per-node fields for sum, best prefix, best suffix, and best subarray; (ii) updates in O(log n) per update; (iii) uses a lazy rebuild every T batches to amortize work; (iv) uses near-linear memory; (v) includes two optimizations and a comparison to recomputing from scratch after every batch. Provide asymptotics in n, B, T?
