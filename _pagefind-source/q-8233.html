Consider five boolean variables A,B,C,D,E with values in {T, F, U}. Constraints: (A ∧ B) → C, (B ∨ D) → E, (¬E) → (A ∨ C), (D → ¬A). Is there an assignment with at least one U such that none of the constraints evaluates to F under Kleene three-valued logic? Provide an incremental 3-valued backtracking propagation approach and a concrete satisfying valuation?
