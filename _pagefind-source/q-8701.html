You have an array a[1..n]. You receive online batches of B point updates (a[i] = v) while preserving the sequence order. After each batch, output the length of the Longest Increasing Subsequence (LIS) of a. Propose a practical batched data structure that (i) partitions the array into blocks of size K ≈ sqrt(n), (ii) stores per-block LIS profile to enable fast cross-block LIS composition, (iii) supports incremental updates and amortized rebuilds, (iv) adds two optimizations, and (v) compares to recomputing LIS from scratch after every batch. Provide preprocessing time, memory, and asymptotics in n, B, K?
