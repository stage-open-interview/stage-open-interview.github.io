In a policy evaluation engine, six predicates P1..P6 take values in {T, F, U}. Under Kleene three-valued logic (¬T=F, ¬F=T, ¬U=U; ∧/∨ use min/max with T>U>F), evaluate these constraints: (P1 ∧ P2) → P3, (P3 ∨ P4) → P5, (¬P6) ∨ P1, and (P2 ∧ ¬P5) → P6. Does there exist an assignment with at least one U such that none evaluates to F? Outline a backtracking propagation strategy and give a concrete satisfying assignment?
