Two-cycle temporal logic puzzle: four propositions P1..P4 take values in {T, F, U}. Over cycles c0 and c1, constraints: (P1@c0 ∨ P2@c1) → P3@c1, (P2@c0 ∧ P4@c0) → ¬P1@c1, (P3@c0) → (P4@c1 ∨ P1@c1). Is there an assignment with at least one U across both cycles such that none of the constraints evaluates to F? Outline a simple 3-valued backtracking propagation to verify arbitrary such rule sets and supply a concrete satisfying assignment?
