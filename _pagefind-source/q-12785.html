You maintain a dynamic set of 2D points; insertions occur in batches of size B with no deletions. After each batch, update the convex hull and answer: (i) is the hull strictly convex (no collinear consecutive hull vertices)? (ii) hull area? (iii) does area exceed threshold A? Propose a practical data structure that partitions points into blocks, keeps a local hull per block, and a global hull formed by merging block hulls. Provide asymptotics for preprocessing, amortized per-batch update, and memory in terms of n=|P|, B, and H (hull size). Compare to rebuilding from scratch?
