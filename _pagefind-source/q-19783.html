Consider four signals A,B,C,D in {T,F,U} modeling a shared bus. The constraints are: (A ∨ B) → C; (¬B ∨ D) ∧ (A → ¬D); (C ∨ ¬A) → (B ∧ D). Is there an assignment with at least one U such that none of the three constraints evaluates to F? Propose a concrete 3-valued backtracking propagation method to verify arbitrary such rule sets and include a small walkthrough?
