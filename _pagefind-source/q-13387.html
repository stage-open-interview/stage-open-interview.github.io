Given a permutation of n distinct integers initialized as [1, 2, ..., n], you apply online batches of B adjacent swaps (swap positions i and i+1). After each batch, output the length of the Longest Increasing Subsequence (LIS) of the current permutation. Propose a practical data structure to maintain LIS length under batched adjacent swaps, with (i) update time per batch, (ii) space, (iii) comparison to recomputing LIS from scratch after each batch, and (iv) concrete asymptotics in terms of n and B?
