In a data structure storing n keys, arrivals happen only in batches of size B. You need insert and point-lookup operations. Strategy A: insert each key with standard rebalancing (O(log n) per insert, O(log n) per lookup). Strategy B: after every batch, rebuild into a perfectly balanced B-ary tree (height O(log_B n)). Derive the amortized cost per insert and per lookup for both strategies, discuss cache effects, and give asymptotics in terms of n and B?
