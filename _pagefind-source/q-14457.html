In Belnap's four-valued logic, values are {T, F, B, N}. Define ¬, ∧, ∨ and → with standard Belnap semantics (¬ swaps T/F, B stays B, N stays N; ∧ as information meet, ∨ as information join; A→B = ¬A ∨ B). Constraints: (X1 ∧ X2) → X3, (X2 ∨ X4) → X5, (¬X5) → (X1 ∨ X4). Is there an assignment with at least one N such that none of the three constraints evaluates to F? Propose a concrete assignment and a propagation strategy?
